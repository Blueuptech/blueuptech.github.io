## University Performance

Reflecting on my university performance, the initial years of my Computer Science degree were notably distinct due to the shared foundational courses with other science disciplines, such as Chemistry and Physics. These foundational years were heavily focused on Analysis, Geometry, and Probability. It wasn't until the third year that I was able to delve into programming, game theory, and other critical subjects that were pivotal in shaping my professional pathway. Here’s an overview of my academic journey, highlighting the evolution of my skills and how these experiences contributed to my career:

- **Foundational Courses**:
  - **Analysis, Geometry, and Probability**: The first two years were dedicated to building a solid foundation in these core subjects. While these courses were challenging, they laid the groundwork for critical thinking and problem-solving skills that would be essential in my later studies.
  - **Transition to Specialized Courses**: In my third year, I finally had the opportunity to engage with programming, game theory, and a wide array of courses that were crucial for my development. These subjects allowed me to construct the framework necessary for my professional advancement.

- **Challenges and Adaptations**:
  - **Early Work Experience**: Tragically, the death of my father necessitated that I start working before completing my degree. However, this period also presented a unique opportunity to work with emerging eBusiness technologies, which were groundbreaking in 1998. This experience not only provided me with practical application of my academic knowledge but also positioned me at the forefront of technology during that era.
  
- **Impact on Career**:
  - **Practical Application of Theoretical Knowledge**: Despite the early transition to the workforce, the practical application of my studies in real-world scenarios greatly enhanced my understanding and skills in technology. This blend of academic knowledge and work experience was instrumental in shaping my career path.
  - **Adapting to Professional Challenges**: The necessity to work while studying introduced me to the challenges of balancing academic and professional responsibilities. This experience honed my time management and organizational skills, proving to be invaluable throughout my career.

- **Reflections on Success**:
  - **Engagement with Coursework**: Despite the unexpected shift in my academic and professional life, my engagement with my coursework and the ability to apply theoretical concepts in practical settings were key factors in my success.
  - **Navigating Challenges**: The challenges I faced, including the need to work early in my career, taught me resilience and adaptability. These attributes have been crucial in navigating the rapidly evolving tech landscape.

My journey through university and into the early stages of my career highlights the importance of adaptability, practical application of academic knowledge, and the ability to overcome personal and professional challenges. These experiences have not only contributed to my academic and professional development but have also prepared me for future opportunities and challenges in the technology field.

## High School Performance

Reflecting on my high school years, my academic journey in mathematics, physical sciences, and computing was both rewarding and enlightening, laying the foundation for my future interests and career path. Here’s a breakdown of my performance and interests in these subjects:

- **Mathematics**: Mathematics was always a strength for me, thanks to my analytical mindset and problem-solving skills. I enjoyed the logical structure and the satisfaction of solving complex problems. Competitively, I ranked in the upper percentile of my class.

- **Physical Sciences**: My performance in physical sciences, including physics and chemistry, was strong. I was fascinated by the practical applications of theoretical concepts, which made these subjects enjoyable. In terms of competition, I was among the top performers, especially in physics, which aligned closely with my interests in understanding the fundamental laws of nature.

- **Computing**: Computing was not only a strength but also the most enjoyable subject for me. The process of coding, problem-solving, and seeing my ideas come to life through programs was immensely satisfying. My competitive ranking in computing was high, as I often excelled in projects and coding competitions. This subject captivated my interest the most, steering my academic and career interests towards technology and software development.

In summary, while I found strengths and enjoyment in all three areas, Maths and computing stood out as the subject that not only piqued my interest the most but also shaped my future direction. The blend of analytical challenges in mathematics, the exploratory nature of physical sciences, and the creative and logical processes in computing provided a well-rounded educational experience that has been instrumental in my development.

# High School Student Profile

 I can characterize myself as a multifaceted student who balanced academic pursuits with a rich array of extracurricular activities and interests. Here’s an overview of my high school student profile:

- **Academically Engaged**: In the classroom, I was known for my curiosity and engagement. Always eager to learn, I participated actively in discussions and was keen on delving deeper into subjects that caught my interest, especially in the fields of computing, science, and mathematics.

- **Extracurricular Involvement**: Outside of academics, I was deeply involved in extracurricular activities that broadened my skills and interests but not directly linked to my studies, as estuding music at Real Conservatorio de Madrid and active participation in my church.

- **Creative Pursuits**: My interest in creativity found an outlet in music. I played the guitar in the school band, sharing my passion for music with peers and participating in school concerts and events. 

- **Community Service**: Volunteering was an important aspect of my high school years. I engaged in community service projects, such as tutoring younger students and participating in environmental clean-ups, which instilled a sense of responsibility and community engagement.

- **Peer Relationships**: Among my peers, I would be remembered as a supportive and collaborative student. I enjoyed working on group projects and was always willing to lend a hand or offer advice. My enthusiasm for sharing knowledge and helping others likely left a lasting impression.

If my high school peers were asked about me, they would likely recall a student who was not only dedicated to his studies but also actively involved in the church, music, and community, passionate about technology and creativity, and always ready to contribute positively to those around him.

## University Choice

When it was time to choose a degree and university for my higher education, the decision was influenced by a combination of my academic interests, career aspirations, and the reputation of the institutions under consideration. However, the choice was also significantly shaped by the lack of private universities at the time and a predetermined university option based on my place of residence. Here's a detailed look at my choice and the rationale behind it:

- **Degree Selection**: Initially, I chose to pursue a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics with a focus on Computer Science. This decision was influenced by the fact that the year I started my higher education was the first year the Complutense University of Madrid offered a degree in Computer Science. Traditionally, those interested in computing had to opt for degrees in Telecommunications Engineering, Mathematics, or Physics. My strong interest in technology, programming, and the potential impact of software development on solving real-world problems led me to Mathematics, as it was closely related to Computer Science. This field promised a blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills, essential for a career in technology and software development.

- **University Choice**: Given the constraints of location and the absence of private universities, my choice was limited. I decided on the Complutense University of Madrid, influenced by several factors:
  - **Academic Excellence**: While not originally my first choice, the university is known for its solid academic programs, especially in the fields of Mathematics and, more recently, Computer Science. Its programs are designed to provide a strong foundation in theoretical knowledge and practical skills.
  - **Research Opportunities**: Despite the initial absence of a dedicated Computer Science program, the university offered opportunities to engage in research and projects that allowed me to explore my interest in technology and its applications.
  - **Community and Culture**: The university's community of peers and faculty provided a supportive environment for learning and collaboration, which was crucial for my personal and professional growth.
  

The combination of academic rigor, a supportive community, and the prospects for personal and professional growth at the Complutense University of Madrid made it the right choice for pursuing my studies in Mathematics, with a focus on Computer Science. This decision reflected my ambition to contribute to technological advancements and shape the future of society, despite the initial constraints related to university and degree options.

## University Performance

The initial years of my Computer Science degree were notably distinct due to the shared foundational courses with other science disciplines, such as Chemistry and Physics. These foundational years were heavily focused on Analysis, Geometry, and Probability. It wasn't until the third year that I was able to delve into programming, logic, and other critical subjects that were pivotal in shaping my professional pathway. 

- **Challenges and Adaptations**:
  - **Early Work Experience**: Tragically, the death of my father necessitated that I start working before completing my degree. However, this period also presented a unique opportunity to work with emerging eBusiness technologies, which were groundbreaking in 1998. This experience not only provided me with practical application of my academic knowledge but also positioned me at the forefront of technology during that era.
 

My journey through university and into the early stages of my career highlights the importance of adaptability, practical application of academic knowledge, and the ability to overcome personal and professional challenges. These experiences have not only contributed to my academic and professional development but have also prepared me for future opportunities and challenges in the technology field.

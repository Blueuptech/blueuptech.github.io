# 📈 Growth and Resilience in the Cybersecurity Market

In recent months, I have had the opportunity and necessity to observe more closely how the cybersecurity community is developing. This is not only in terms of market volume compared to other types of projects or services, but also in its relevance to the business, training offerings, and the creation of new products and companies. Despite the increasing challenges, in all relevant indicators, we have experienced clear and evident growth and improvement. Companies specializing in cybersecurity have felt the effects of economic crises minimally, and for those who choose the path of entrepreneurship, there is a considerable volume of investment available for our sector.

## 🌐 Impact of Digital Transformation

Companies that effectively integrate cybersecurity into their digital transformation processes not only achieve more efficient transformations, but also improve their competitiveness and market growth.

## 🚀 Strategic Importance of Cybersecurity

Cybersecurity is gaining strategic relevance within companies, increasingly being included in board discussions. This integration is crucial for better risk management and optimizing business operations.

From an institutional perspective, it has long been recommended that cybersecurity be represented on the board of directors. Currently, there is national discussion about the most appropriate governance for this, but the intention to promote cybersecurity initiatives is clear:

- **Awareness and Responsibility**: It is recommended that at least one board member has experience in cybersecurity management. Continuous training and awareness in cybersecurity are fundamental to strengthening protection against cyberattacks.

- **NIS2 Directive**: The transposition of this directive establishes a framework for managing cybersecurity at the national and European level, including the creation of a coordination body to improve resilience against cyber risks.

## 📉 Challenges for Cybersecurity Professionals

Despite growing recognition, the institutional weight of CISOs often does not reflect proportionally in organizations, which can result in a lack of complete visibility over the organization's security posture. Moreover, there is significant pressure on the costs of professionals, highlighting the importance of specialization in areas such as pentesting and cybersecurity, beyond conventional outsourcing strategies.
